TEXT ANNOTATION: TEACHING AI HUMAN MEANING

In human communication, one sentence can carry many meanings. A person may say “I am fine”, but the real feeling behind the words may be sadness, tiredness, anger, fear, or silent frustration. This is why text annotation is important. It helps AI understand not just the words, but the emotion, tone, intent, and context behind the message.

When someone writes, “This is terrible. I am very disappointed.”, the annotator may label it as negative emotion, complaint, frustration, and dissatisfaction. But when another person says, “Thank you so much, this really helped me.”, the sentence can be labeled as positive emotion, gratitude, satisfaction, and appreciation.

Text annotation also teaches AI how to notice action. Words like request, complain, ask, warn, and recommend show what the person is trying to do with the message.

A simple sentence like “Can you please fix this today?” may show urgency, request, politeness, and expectation. Another sentence like “If this happens again, I will stop using your service.” may show warning, anger, risk, and customer dissatisfaction.

This is how AI developers train systems to understand people better. They use human annotators to label feelings, actions, emotions, tone, risk, and meaning. Without this human work, AI would only read words without understanding the deeper message behind them.
